Welcome to the Phenoscape Knowledgebase (or KB), a datastore of computable phenotypes for studies of evolution and genetics. You can explore a large number of manually curated annotations in the KB derived from the literature. These annotations, taken from trusted literature sources, report that a taxon (or genotype) shows variation in some quality (e.g. shape, size, etc.) in some entity (anatomical part), using controlled vocabulary terms from community ontologies that allow for semantic reasoning across diverse phenotypes from different organisms.

Much of the recent evolutionary data in the KB is from the appendicular skeleton of sarcopterygian vertebrates, the clade in which the “fin to limb” transition occurred. Data from the first iteration of the KB, with broad coverage of ostariophysan fishes, is included here as well, and also available via the previous KB software. Phenotype data from gene disruptions have been imported for mouse (from MGI), human (HPO), zebrafish (ZFIN), and frog (Xenbase).

New features available in the KB include:

  • Generation of synthetic presence/absence comparative data matrices (Dececchi et al., 2015)
  • Semantic similarity search for evolutionary variation related to gene phenotypes (Manda et al., 2015, Balhoff et al., 2015)

Find phenotypic data for an anatomical structure

An anatomical ontology puts phenotypic data into a rich anatomical and developmental context.

Find taxa with phenotypic data for any type of endochondral bone. Or genes from mouse, zebrafish, frog, or human with phenotypic effects.

Find evolutionary variation similar to genetic phenotypes

Using semantic similarity, the KB can perform fuzzy matching between suites of phenotypes, such as the various phenotypic effects of a gene vs. the profile of phenotypic variation within a given taxonomic group.

View taxonomic variation similar to the phenotypic profile of the gene apa.

View presence and absence of any anatomical structure across taxa

Using the OntoTrace reasoning framework, the KB infers presence of absence of anatomical structure from both direct and indirect statements.

The pectoral fin is known to be present in some taxa, but absent in others. You can also view the phenotypic assertions leading to an inference, such as absence of pectoral fin in Branchiostoma.

Download a character matrix of presence/absence inferred knowledge

Configure a NeXML character matrix download for any type of anatomical structure and taxonomic group using the OntoTrace page.

For example, you can create a character matrix of presence/absence variation for any fin structures and their parts for Ostariophysan fishes.
